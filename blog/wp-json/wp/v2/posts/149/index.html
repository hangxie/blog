I got problem with my KVM cable – the mouse was having problem to keep connection with the KVM adapter so it stopped working from time to time, and because of the adapter is sort of loose so I have to unplug-plug for couple of times to make it work.<\/p>\n

So I opened ticket to IT, somebody pinged me this morning and we started trying to figure out what’s the problem and what’s the best way to solve it.<\/p>\n

Then, the IT guy asked me, “How many devices are you currently connecting to the KVM?”, I said “two but I’m using only one of them as I need to have both desktop and laptop screens at the same time” …<\/p>\n

WAIT – suddenly I found how stupid I am – I actually don’t need the KVM at all, I can just pull it out and throw away, and let keyboard\/mouse and monitor connect to the RHEL box directly, so that I don’t have to worry about any problem from KVM at all.<\/p>\n

So I told him what I want to do, and quick the live chat (yea, live chat is better than phone call), unplug mouse and keyboard from KVM, plug into the RHEL box, everything is set.<\/p>\n

I have to say, sometime people just get into the corner, this is why there is always the training of “Think out of box”. \ud83d\ude00<\/p>\n","protected":false},"excerpt":{"rendered":"
